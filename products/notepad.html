Recycled Notepad


Product Description

Manufacturing & Materials: Our eco-friendly spiral notepads are made from 100% post-consumer recycled paper collected, cleaned, and repurposed through a modern pulping and de-inking process. These sturdy notepads reuse old office papers, newspapers, and cardboard that would otherwise pile up in landfills. The process breaks down paper fibers, removes inks and waste, and reforms them into smooth, high-quality sheets. The recycled pulp is bleached with oxygen-based, chlorine-free methods and pressed into strong, opaque sheets perfect for writing.

Features & Benefits: Ideal for notes, sketches, journaling, or office tasks, these spiral notepads have tough wire-o binding and perforated pages for easy, clean removal. They come in many sizes, from pocket-sized to letter format, and can be customized with eco-friendly soy ink covers. The spiral design lets pages fold back fully for one-handed writing, and the solid recycled cardboard back offers firm support. These notepads replace hundreds of single-use sheets and sticky notes, cutting paper waste and supporting a circular economy by turning old waste into everyday essentials.

Environmental Impact

Paper Waste Reduction

Each notepad is made from approximately 200-300 sheets of discarded office paper, newspapers, and cardboard, preventing them from entering landfills or contributing to deforestation.

Carbon Footprint

Reduces CO2 emissions by 65% compared to manufacturing new notepads from virgin wood pulp and eliminates the need for tree harvesting.

Resource Conservation

Uses 45% less energy and 35% less water in production versus creating new paper from trees, while preserving natural forest ecosystems.

Circular Economy

Creates a closed-loop system where waste paper materials become new functional writing products, extending the lifecycle of paper fibers.

Lifetime Impact

Replaces up to 500 loose sheets and sticky notes over its lifespan, while each notepad saves approximately 0.5 trees from being cut down for paper production.

Waste paper is pulped, de-inked, and pressed into smooth sheets using an oxygen-based, chlorine-free process. This maintains paper strength and writing quality.

A light water-based coating improves durability and writing comfort without harming recyclability. No harmful chemicals like phthalates or chlorine are used.
